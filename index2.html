Schneider Electric - Estimating Software iPad and Web App

Schneider Electric one of the worlds biggest electrical manufacturers needed to replace their outdated Lotus Notes estimating software. The system needed to allow service engineers to create and manage quotes and be synced on both a native iPad app and a web app supported by internal administrators and managers.




My Role

I was responsible for the collection and the understanding of business requirements, user research, prototyping and design of both the iPad and Web app UI/UX.

In addition to leading the UX process, I also guided a team of three developers thought the full software development life cycle using JIRA sprint boards and the agile methodology.

Problem Statement

Lotus Notes was introduced into the company decades ago. At the time it proved useful for emails, notes, todo lists and other business processes so it was decided that it would be used for the estimating of service repairs for existing clients. This included quotes for material, labour and delivery.

Many of the customers have service level agreements and terms specific to contracts such as discount pricing and markups.

As Lotus Notes was never designed for this purpose, the internal staff took it upon themselves to build macros and flesh out the gaps. The system had no materials database but would instead require a user to input the product code, description, price, markup, discount and delivery cost of each material needed for the job.

This was made even more difficult by the fact that service engineers are based on the road and at the time only provided with 13in Dell Thinkpads. They also needed to dial into a secure FTP server to access Lotus Notes, which proved extremely difficult as some sites strictly forbid the use of the Internet for national security reasons.

This leads to inaccuracies, inefficiencies and loss of revenue.

The system also needed to work with Schneider Electric’s two-factor authentication server Ping ID.

Project Goals

Move away from Lotus Notes

Consult with service engineers to identify problems

Speed up and improve the quote process

Access quotes offline

Implement two-factor authentication

Sync quotes with SAP

Give management better business intelligence


My Process

  • Understand

    I started by first looking at the existing system and making a mind map using MindView. This helped me prepare detailed interview questions for each of the stockholders.


    Interviews were conducted onsite and record for later reference. I was then provided with technical documentation and a survey of service engineers opinion of the existing tool.


    After reviewing the material I ran an affinity diagram session to cluster and bundle my ideas as outlined in the image below.

    I used post-it notes, and the Persuasive Patterns - Card Deck for this process.


  • Define

    After performing my initial research I used Adobe XD to create personas of each of the stakeholders to help me keep the users always in my mind throughout the process.


    I then used Adobe XD to create a high-level user flow of a service engineer making a simple quote and sending to a customer. This helped me define goals, behaviours and pain points for each of the steps as outlined in the image below.


  • Ideate

    I then used A4 plain paper and a sharpie pen to sketch out the main screens I knew I would need such as home, project details, quote, catalogue, send screen and existing quotes page and stuck them to my wall.

    I then used Draw.io and Adobe XD to iterate and to test ideas with the team, and then I created a low fidelity flow diagram as outlined in the image below.


  • Prototype

    I then used Adobe XD to create a medium-fidelity prototype of the main user flows for each of the personas and screen sizes.


    We held weekly sprint meetings with the clients to show progress and get constant feedback.


    I then defined a design system using the “Atom” methodology and applied this to the prototype.

    I then used After Effects and Premiere Pro to create a video demonstration of my thinking to major stakeholders for initial feedback.


  • Validate

    Once I had created the prototype I then define test cases and performed user testing with individuals that I recorded using Camtasia and large focus groups of around 30 service engineers.

    I did this using Adobe XD’s live preview feature that allows you to connect as many devices as you want and gives the user the ability to play with the prototype while allowing me to take back control if the user ran into any difficulty.

  • Build

    After iterating on the prototype and design I used Adobe XD, Confluence and JIRA to hand off my design to the software development team.


    I then facilitated daily stand up’s and weekly sprints with the software development team to make sure we met our major milestone.


    I also worked with the client to feedback weekly progress and to facilitate system integration with existing enterprise systems such as authentication server, SAP and IT security.


    We delivered the project as expected ready for acceptance testing.

  • Test

    The software development team used test-driven development to deliver both applications.

    I then helped Schneider Electric organise user acceptance testing to identify any issues. I then worked with software developers to remediate and then retest .

Learnings

It was clear from my research with the field service engineers that offline access and the speed of quote creation was their top priority. To achieve this we built a background downloading/syncing that made sure when they arrived at the site they had everything they needed.


In addition, we reduced the number of inputs needed by 80% by introducing context-aware defaults and adding features such as “Recently used materials”


It was also clear that management needs to be able to have full visibility of each of the regions and users performance via the web app version of the tool.
